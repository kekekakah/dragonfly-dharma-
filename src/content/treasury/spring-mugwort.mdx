  <div class="article-section__label">
    <span class="article-section__label-zh">经典解读</span>
    <span class="article-section__label-en">Reading</span>
  </div>
  <div class="article-section__content">
    Mugwort (*Artemisia argyi*, or 艾叶 *Ai Ye*) is intrinsically tied to the transition from winter to spring. As the earth thaws and Yang energy begins to rise, the body can sometimes hold onto the residual cold and dampness of the previous season.

    Classical texts describe Mugwort as bitter, acrid, and warm. Its warmth is not a superficial heat, but a penetrating one that enters the Spleen, Liver, and Kidney meridians. By "regulating Qi and Blood" (理气血) and "dispelling cold and dampness" (逐寒湿), Mugwort acts as an internal cleanser, clearing out stagnation and allowing the body's vital energy to flow freely.

    In the context of seasonal nourishment, consuming Mugwort in early spring—often in the form of Qingtuan (青团, green sticky rice balls) or as an infusion—helps to warm the digestive system and protect the body from late spring chills.

<div class="article-section section-baihua">

    <span class="article-section__label-en">Explanation</span>
  </div>
  <div class="article-section__content">
    艾叶（Ai Ye）与冬春之交的节气息息相关。当大地回暖、阳气生发时，人体内有时仍会残留冬季的寒湿之气。

    古籍记载艾叶味苦、辛，性温。它的温热并非表面的燥热，而是一种能深入脾、肝、肾经的温煦之力。通过“理气血”与“逐寒湿”，艾叶就像是体内的清道夫，能够清除瘀滞，使人体的气血运行畅通。

    在节气食养中，早春食用艾叶（如制作青团或艾叶茶），有助于温润脾胃，抵御“倒春寒”的侵袭，为一年的健康打下基础。

<div class="in-practice">

  <div class="in-practice__content">
    **Spring Mugwort Tea:** For those who feel sluggish or cold in the extremities during early spring, a mild infusion of dried mugwort leaves with a slice of fresh ginger can help gently warm the digestive tract and dispel dampness. Consume in moderation, as its nature is quite warming.

    **春日艾叶茶：** 若在早春感到身体沉重或手脚冰凉，可用少量干艾叶搭配一片生姜泡水饮用。这能温和地暖胃并驱散体内湿气。因艾叶性温，请适量饮用。
